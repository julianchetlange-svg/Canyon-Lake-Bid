Read the divisions together, not in isolation

The two GCs bucketed scope differently. Teal folds all site concrete paving into Division 03, while Teinert breaks it out in Division 32. That alone makes Teal's Div 03 look ~$908K high and its Div 32 look low — pure allocation, not price. See Cost Comparison for the normalized read (true gap ~$209K).

Teinert's lead looks real — confirm a short list.

Teinert is lower at every level of leveling — $534,181 on the raw base bids, $483,108 after each bid's own adds, and $333,108 once rock is applied to both sides. Teinert's detailed breakdown now backs that up: the two biggest feared gaps — the aerobic septic (~$196K) and the TCS controls (~$53K) — are both fully included, and structural steel and exterior glazing are correctly left to the supplier.

The lower number is mostly leaner pricing and lower overhead/fee (~$210K below Teal), not missing scope. The open list is short: Teinert's rock-excavation basis (shown "included" with no allowance, where Teal hedges with $150K), its small monument-masonry and HVAC-commissioning exclusions, a Teal-side question on interior drywall ($64K Teinert itemizes that Teal doesn't show), and re-pricing Teal at the 10-month schedule (its 12-month plan runs ~3 months past McCoy's own baseline turnover (4/26/27, from the completed New Caney #121), driven by a 75-day rock activity both GCs could likely beat).

Net: this is closer to a real ~$333–483K advantage than a scope mirage. Resolve the rock basis and the Teal drywall line — see Next Steps — and the comparison is largely firm.

Where the ~3 months live

McCoy's own baseline — built from a store that actually delivered — turns the building over to store planning on 4/26/27, about three months ahead of Teal's 7/23 substantial completion. So the extra time in Teal's plan reads as conservatism, not site necessity (pending the Canyon Lake rock confirmation). Three things stretch Teal to 12: a 75-day strip & rock-excavation activity at the front of the critical path (the same conservatism behind its $150K rock allowance), largely sequential sitework (strip → utilities → subgrade → paving runs ~9 months end to end), and an 80-day interior buildout that overlaps PEMB erection by only 10 days.

Two scheduling levers that touch the money

  1. 01

    The rock assumption drives both price and schedule

    The 75-day strip/rock-excavation activity and the $150K rock allowance are the same conservatism expressed two ways. Teinert prices rock leaner (shown "included") and bids 10 months — near McCoy's baseline turnover (~9.5 months). Confirming the rock basis settles ~$150K of cost and ~2 months of schedule at once.

  2. 02

    The locked delivery exposes a Teal sequencing gap

    The building is locked for delivery 10/5–10/19 — McCoy's standard ~90-days-in timing. But Teal's CPM doesn't have a slab to erect on until 2/1/27, so the steel would sit on site ~3.5 months. That's not a delivery risk — it's a sequencing question: ask Teal to get foundations and slab ready to erect shortly after the locked delivery, not in February. It also raises a staging/storage question — the PEMB is owner-furnished, so the hold is likely McCoy's risk (weather, damage, laydown) until it's erected.

  3. 03

    Time is money: ~$85–125K of general conditions

    Teal's time-related general conditions run ~$42K/month. Teal lands ~3 months past McCoy's baseline turnover and ~2 past Teinert's bid — so compressing toward the 10-month range pulls roughly $85–125K of GCs and aligns the opening date with Teinert's. Worth real money either way.
