Read the divisions together, not in isolation

The two GCs bucketed scope differently. Teal folds all site concrete paving into Division 03, while Teinert breaks it out in Division 32. That alone makes Teal's Div 03 look ~$908K high and its Div 32 look low — pure allocation, not price. See Cost Comparison for the normalized read (true gap ~$209K).

Don't award on price alone yet.

Teinert is lower at every level of leveling — $534,181 on the raw base bids, $483,108 after each bid's own adds, and $333,108 once rock is applied to both sides. That ordering is consistent, so Teinert is genuinely the lower number.

But the spread lives almost entirely in Divisions 03, 31, 32 and 33. The responsibility matrix clears most of the earlier "confirm" flags — PEMB, steel, glazing, façade and fire systems are supplier and vendor scope, correctly excluded by both. The open list is now short: Teinert's TCS controls, septic and monument masonry (all GC scope per the matrix), the earthwork reconciliation, and small true-ups on Teal (add the yard bell, deduct the fire specialties).

Recommended next step: a formal scope-leveling session on concrete, earthwork, paving and utilities — see Next Steps for the clarifications to send each GC.

Where to focus before award

  1. 01

    The matrix clears most "by Owner" lines

    PEMB & erection, structural/decorative steel, exterior glazing, rock façade, fire suppression, fire alarm/security, branding signage and rough carpentry are all assigned to the supplier (Sunbelt/CT Darnell), 1st Fire Protection, Highpoint or McCoy's Carpenters. Both GCs correctly exclude them — these are not gaps.

  2. 02

    Confirmed Teinert scope gaps

    The matrix makes TCS/EMS controls (~$53K) and the aerobic septic system (~$196K, Davis Septic) GC scope, so Teinert omitting either is a real gap — not a saving. Plumbing ($62K vs $112K) and earthwork (-$308K) remain to be reconciled. Teinert's monument-sign masonry exclusion is also a likely gap, since that assembly is GC scope.

  3. 03

    Two small corrections on Teal

    Teal carries ~$8K of Owner scope (Knox Box + fire extinguishers belong to 1st Fire Protection) and excluded the yard bell that the matrix makes GC scope — roughly a wash, but both should be trued up.

  4. 04

    It's not a uniformly lean bid

    Teinert is higher on HVAC (+$95K), Finishes (+$95K) and Masonry (+$77K). With exterior steel and glazing being supplier scope, Teinert's higher Metals and Openings are worth a second look to confirm no supplier scope is double-carried.

  5. 05

    Level the risk items

    Teal recommends a $150K rock allowance but carries no contingency; Teinert carries 3% contingency but no rock allowance. On equal footing, Teinert is still ~$333K lower.

  6. 06

    Mind the shared exclusions

    Both exclude the P&P bond (not in the matrix — an Owner decision). Add bond cost to both bids before comparing final award numbers.

Clarifications to request before award

A leveled award decision needs these answered by both GCs — most of the apparent spread lives in the first two items.

  1. 1

    Division 03 — concrete reconciliation

    Get each GC's full Div 03 + paving breakout so the normalized ~$209K gap can be verified, line by line:

    • Building foundations & slab
    • Site concrete paving
    • Retaining walls
    • Light-pole bases
    • Transformer pad
    • Canopy & sign foundations
  2. 2

    Divisions 31 / 32 / 33 — site reconciliation

    The earthwork gap (+$308K Teal) and utilities are the next-largest swing. Confirm scope parity on:

    • Rock excavation basis & quantities
    • Subgrade and import/export assumptions
    • Retaining wall scope & height
    • SWPPP and detention pond
    • Aerobic septic system inclusion
    • Landscape & fencing scope
  3. 3

    Close Teinert's gaps — all matrix-confirmed GC scope

    The matrix makes each of these GC scope, so confirm it's carried or price the add:

    • TCS / EMS controls — 26 09 00 (~$53K)
    • Aerobic septic — Davis Septic (~$196K)
    • Monument-sign masonry assembly
    • Plumbing scope ($62K vs Teal's $112K)
  4. 4

    Allowances, risk & schedule

    Normalize the risk terms — and confirm the schedule, since GCs scale with duration:

    • Rock allowance basis (both)
    • Teinert 3% contingency vs. Teal cost-plus-fee escalation
    • True up Teal: add yard bell (GC scope), deduct Knox Box + fire extinguishers (Owner scope)
    • Owner-furnished item delay exposure (Teal adds GCs + markup)
    • P&P bond add if Owner requires
    • Confirm 10-month duration & its impact on Teal's general conditions
    • Mobilization date & long-lead procurement
